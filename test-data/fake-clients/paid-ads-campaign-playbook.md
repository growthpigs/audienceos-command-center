## 1. Campaign Strategy Framework

### 1.1 The Funnel Approach

We structure all campaigns around the customer journey:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    TOF (Top of Funnel)              │
│                    AWARENESS                        │
│              New audiences, cold traffic            │
│                   Goal: Reach/Views                 │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                    MOF (Middle of Funnel)           │
│                    CONSIDERATION                    │
│           Engaged audiences, warm traffic           │
│              Goal: Engagement/Traffic               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                    BOF (Bottom of Funnel)           │
│                    CONVERSION                       │
│          Retargeting, high-intent audiences         │
│           Goal: Purchases/Leads/Sign-ups            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 1.2 Budget Distribution Guidelines

| Client Type | TOF | MOF | BOF |
|-------------|-----|-----|-----|
| Brand Awareness | 60% | 25% | 15% |
| Lead Generation | 40% | 35% | 25% |
| E-commerce | 30% | 30% | 40% |
| Retargeting Focus | 20% | 30% | 50% |

### 1.3 KPI Targets by Objective

| Objective | Primary KPI | Target Range |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Awareness | CPM | $5-15 |
| Traffic | CPC | $0.50-2.00 |
| Engagement | CPE | $0.10-0.50 |
| Leads | CPL | $15-75 (B2B) / $5-25 (B2C) |
| Purchases | ROAS | 2.5x-4x+ |

## 2. Meta Ads Playbook

### 2.1 Campaign Naming Convention

```
[Client]_[Objective]_[Funnel]_[Audience]_[Date]
```

**Examples:**
- `UrbanThread_CONV_BOF_Cart-Abandon_2025-01`
- `CloudMetrics_LEAD_TOF_Lookalike-1%_2025-01`

### 2.2 Audience Strategy

**Cold Audiences (TOF):**
- Interest-based targeting
- Lookalike audiences (1-3%)
- Broad targeting with creative differentiation

**Warm Audiences (MOF):**
- Website visitors (7-30 days)
- Video viewers (50%+)
- Social engagers (90 days)
- Email list matches

**Hot Audiences (BOF):**
- Add to cart (7 days)
- Checkout initiators
- Past purchasers (for upsell)
- High-value page visitors

### 2.3 Ad Set Structure

**Standard Structure:**
- 3-5 ad sets per campaign
- 3-6 ads per ad set
- Minimum $20/day per ad set for learning
- Advantage+ placements (unless testing)

### 2.4 Creative Best Practices

**Image Ads:**
- 1:1 for feed, 9:16 for stories/reels
- Minimal text (under 20% coverage)
- Clear focal point
- Brand colors prominent

**Video Ads:**
- Hook in first 3 seconds
- Subtitles mandatory
- 15-30 seconds optimal
- Clear CTA at end

**Carousel Ads:**
- Tell a story across cards
- Best performer in first position
- 4-6 cards optimal
- Consistent visual style

### 2.5 Bidding Strategy

| Scenario | Bid Strategy |
|----------|--------------|
| New campaign, testing | Lowest cost |
| Scaling, stable performance | Cost cap |
| Limited budget, high competition | Bid cap |
| Maximum volume | Highest volume |

## 3. Google Ads Playbook

### 3.1 Campaign Types & Use Cases

| Campaign Type | Best For | Typical ROAS |
|---------------|----------|--------------|
| Search | High-intent keywords | 4-8x |
| Performance Max | E-commerce | 3-5x |
| Display | Retargeting | 2-4x |
| YouTube | Brand awareness | 1-2x |
| Discovery | Mid-funnel | 2-3x |

### 3.2 Search Campaign Structure

```
Account
└── Campaign: Brand
    └── Ad Group: Brand Terms
        └── Keywords: [brand name], [brand] + product
└── Campaign: Non-Brand - Category
    └── Ad Group: Product Category 1
        └── Keywords: [category] + [intent modifier]
    └── Ad Group: Product Category 2
└── Campaign: Competitor
    └── Ad Group: Competitor Names
```

### 3.3 Keyword Strategy

**Match Types:**
- Exact: [keyword] - Highest intent, lowest volume
- Phrase: "keyword" - Balanced approach
- Broad: keyword - Highest volume, needs monitoring

**Negative Keywords:**
Maintain campaign-level negative lists for:
- Free/cheap modifiers (unless applicable)
- Job-related terms
- DIY terms
- Competitor terms (in brand campaigns)

### 3.4 Shopping / Performance Max

**Feed Optimization:**
- Title: Brand + Product + Key Attribute + Color/Size
- Description: 150+ characters with keywords
- Images: White background, multiple angles
- Price: Competitive, updated daily

**Custom Labels:**
- Margin tiers (high/medium/low)
- Best sellers
- Seasonal items
- Clearance

## 4. Creative Testing Methodology

### 4.1 DCT (Dynamic Creative Testing)

**What to Test:**
1. **Hook** - First 3 seconds / headline
2. **Format** - Image vs. video vs. carousel
3. **Message** - Pain point vs. benefit vs. social proof
4. **CTA** - Shop Now vs. Learn More vs. Get Started
5. **Visual Style** - Lifestyle vs. product vs. UGC

### 4.2 Testing Framework

**Phase 1: Concept Testing**
- Test 4-6 fundamentally different concepts
- Equal budget distribution
- Run for 5-7 days or 50 conversions
- Winner criteria: Lowest CPA at scale

**Phase 2: Iteration Testing**
- Take winning concept
- Create 3-4 variations
- Test different hooks/CTAs/formats
- Identify top performer

**Phase 3: Scale**
- Roll out winner to all audiences
- Create similar variations
- Refresh every 4-6 weeks

### 4.3 Creative Fatigue Signals

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| CTR drops 20%+ | Refresh creative |
| Frequency > 3 | Expand audience or refresh |
| CPM increasing, CTR stable | Audience fatigue |
| Comments turning negative | Major refresh needed |

## 5. Budget Allocation

### 5.1 Starting Budget Framework

**Minimum Viable Budgets:**
| Platform | Monthly Minimum | Recommended |
|----------|-----------------|-------------|
| Meta Ads | $3,000 | $5,000+ |
| Google Search | $2,000 | $4,000+ |
| Google Shopping | $3,000 | $6,000+ |
| YouTube | $2,000 | $5,000+ |

### 5.2 Scaling Rules

**When to Scale:**
- Consistent ROAS above target for 7+ days
- Frequency under 2.5
- CPA stable or improving

**How to Scale:**
- Increase budget by 20% max every 3 days
- Duplicate winning ad sets with new budget
- Expand to new audiences gradually
- Never 2x budget overnight

### 5.3 Budget Reallocation

Weekly review of spend allocation:
- Top performers get 60% of budget
- Mid performers get 30% (optimization zone)
- Bottom performers get 10% (testing zone)

## 6. Optimization Cycles

### 6.1 Daily Checks (15 min)

- [ ] Spend pacing correct?
- [ ] Any campaigns stopped?
- [ ] CPA/ROAS within target?
- [ ] High-frequency alerts?

### 6.2 Weekly Optimization (1 hr)

**Monday:**
- Review weekend performance
- Adjust budgets for the week
- Pause underperformers

**Wednesday:**
- Mid-week performance check
- A/B test analysis
- Bid adjustments

**Friday:**
- Prepare weekend strategy
- Launch new tests
- Update negative keywords

### 6.3 Monthly Deep Dive (2-3 hrs)

1. **Performance Analysis**
   - Month-over-month trends
   - Best/worst performers
   - Audience insights

2. **Creative Review**
   - Top performing creatives
   - Fatigue assessment
   - New concept ideation

3. **Strategy Adjustment**
   - Budget reallocation
   - Audience expansion/refinement
   - Platform mix optimization

## Appendix: Platform-Specific Tips

### Meta Ads

- Use Advantage+ Shopping for e-commerce
- Test Reels placement separately
- Advantage+ Audience often outperforms manual
- Use catalog ads for retargeting

### Google Ads

- Enable auto-apply recommendations selectively
- Use Performance Max asset groups strategically
- Responsive Search Ads need 10+ headlines
- Shopping feed quality = campaign performance

### LinkedIn Ads (B2B)

- Job title targeting > company size
- Lead Gen Forms convert better than landing pages
- Document Ads for thought leadership
- Minimum $50/day per campaign
